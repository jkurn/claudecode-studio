# Stephen Coleman — The harness is where the value is

## Who

- Based in Melbourne.
- 15+ years corporate.
- Founder of Stratum, an AI advisory, ~6 months in market. "Going really
  well."
- Self-described non-technical founder.

## The talk

The epiphany came with Opus 4.6. "I'd find myself up at 2am on a
Thursday morning going 'this is amazing.'"

Central claim: **the value is in the harness, not the model.** Model
improvements have become incremental; harness improvements (Claude Code,
Claude Co-work, MCP, connectors) are still exponential.

TMNT-Krang analogy:

- **Brain in a jar (Krang)** = the model. Capable, but useless alone.
- **Robot body** = the harness.
- **Weapons** = tools (MCPs, data connections, automations).
- **Context** = prompts, skills, memory — what the pilot brings.

Interface evolution:

1. Fat apps (Excel, SAP).
2. SaaS (browser).
3. GaaS — "harness as interface." Claude is the UI; systems connect in
   via MCP/API.

Signal: Benioff announced a headless Salesforce. "If Salesforce is doing
it, everyone follows."

Downstream implication: "The days of building dashboards being the thing
clients are looking for are going to decrease." The consulting pitch
flips from "here's your dashboard" to "here's your MCP-accessible data
layer."

Adoption reality check: dot visualisation where each dot = 3.2M people.
The vast majority have never meaningfully used AI. The slice paying for
Claude and building scaffolding is microscopic.

Junior-consultant anecdote: used Claude to generate a 28-page work plan
when a two-page activities/weeks/deliverables doc was asked for. "The
new literacy is curation of AI output."

## Takeaways for the site

- "Value is in the harness, not the model" is the single best line to
  explain *why* claudecode.studio exists — the whole site is about
  harness fluency.
- The Krang metaphor is sticky and shareable. Worth a standalone
  explainer on the site or an illustrated social post.
- "Taste and judgement are the moat" connects the fluency framework to
  a career argument: L1→L5 isn't just skill, it's trained judgement.
- The adoption dot chart is exactly the reassurance L1/L2 readers need.
  "You're not late. 99% haven't started."
